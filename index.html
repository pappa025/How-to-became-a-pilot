First Steps to your PPL

The first step in your bumpy journey to be a private pilot is to get yourself to a nearby airstrip and take a trial lesson. A trial lesson will give you a good idea what you can expect. It is also a good opportunity to ask your flying instructor questions about the different routes to obtaining your goal. These lessons are available all over the country from larger training organizations like National Flight Centre and Atlantic Training Academy to local smaller flying clubs. Some of the Flying clubs are Designated Training Organizations (DTOs) who can facilitate your jour training towards your Private Pilot License (PPL). All above PPL level needs to be done at an Approved Training Organization (ATO).

The Medical

Though there are three types of medical available in Europe (Class 1, Class 2 and LAPL (light Aircraft Pilot License), for your PPL you will need Class 2 towards CPL (Commercial Pilot License) Class 1 medical. These medicals taking place in an Aeromedical Centre (like at Mater Private Hospital or Charter Medical Group in Dublin). When you pass your medical you’ll need to keep it valid by renewing it each year. The renewal is simpler and quicker than the initial. ECG’s, Hearing tests and Eye tests are conducted every few years depending on your age. Despite popular belief, pilots can wear glasses and the exact requirements can be found in a document called EASA Part-Med

Private Pilot License

Actual flying begin eventually! This is the time when you learn the basic aircraft handling, flying your first solo and cross country flight (at least 150 nautical miles) after you’ve passed 9 multiple choice exams in the subjects Air Law, Operational Procedures, Performance, Navigation, Aircraft Technical Knowledge, Principles of Flight, Communications, Meteorology and Human Performance & Limitations. All exams carry a pass mark of 75%. A General Flight Test with an Irish Aviation Authority Examiner then needs to be passed to obtain your PPL. If all went well you can fly all over Ireland with your newly obtained license!

Night Qualification

A night qualification takes 5 hours and must be completed at a controlled airport (Dublin, Cork, Shannon or any of the regional airports) with runway lighting. One of these hours must be solo and must include 5 take offs and landings. The remaining hours must include cross country navigation and dual circuits.
