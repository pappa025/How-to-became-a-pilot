First Steps to your PPL

The first step in your bumpy journey to be a private pilot is to get yourself to a nearby airstrip and take a trial lesson. A trial lesson will give you a good idea what you can expect. It is also a good opportunity to ask your flying instructor questions about the different routes to obtaining your goal. These lessons are available all over the country from larger training organizations like NFC and AFTA to local smaller flying clubs.

The Medical

Though there are three types of medical available in Europe (Class 1, Class 2 and LAPL (light Aircraft Pilot License), for your PPL you will need Class 2 medical. These medicals taking place in an Aeromedical Centre (like at Mater Private Hospital. When you pass your medical you will need to keep it valid by renewing it each year. The renewal is simpler and quicker than the initial. Despite popular belief, pilots can wear glasses!

Private Pilot License

Actual flying begin eventually! This is the time when you learn the basic aircraft handling, flying your first solo and cross country flight (at least 150 nautical miles) after you have passed 9 multiple choice exams at the IAA. the whole trasining ends with a General Flight Test with an Irish Aviation Authority Examiner! If all went well you can fly all over Ireland with your newly obtained license!
